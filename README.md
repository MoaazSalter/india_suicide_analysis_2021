# India Suicide Analysis 2021

Exploratory data analysis of suicide records in India for the year 2021. The project examines how demographic and socioeconomic factors — marital status, income level, educational attainment, method, and more.

## Dataset

- `Cause-wise Distribution_2021.csv` — cause and age-group breakdown
- `Cause-wise Distribution_economic_status_2021.csv` — income and personal status breakdown
- `Cause-wise Distribution_mode_2021.csv` — method of suicide breakdown

## Analysis

The notebook covers data cleaning, feature engineering, and visualization across five dimensions.

**Marital Status** — 66.9% of the 164,033 recorded deaths were married individuals, with men accounting for 73.9% of married cases. This points to relationship stress and domestic conflict as significant contributing factors rather than social isolation alone.

**Economic Status** — Over 64% of cases involved individuals with an annual income below ₹1 lakh (100,000 INR), while those earning above ₹10 lakhs represented less than 0.8% of total cases, illustrating a strong inverse relationship between income level and suicide risk.

**Educational Status** — Youth in secondary school account for the highest single group at 24% of total cases, reflecting the acute pressures faced during adolescence and the importance of early-stage mental health intervention.

**Methods** — Hanging is the predominant method at 57%, followed by poisoning at 25%. Firearm use is negligible at 0.24%. Gender-specific method distributions are visualized for each category.

**Causes** — Family problems are the leading reported cause. Age-stratified trend lines reveal how cause profiles shift across the lifespan from adolescence through old age.
